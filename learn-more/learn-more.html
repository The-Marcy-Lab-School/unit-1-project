IUD

An IUD (intrauterine device) is inserted directly into the uterus. The hormonal type can last up to 3-5 years, and the copper type can last up to 10 years. The hormonal type releases hormones to prevent pregnancy, while copper is a natural spermicide and will kill any sperm that may enter the uterus.

Effectiveness: 99%

Prevents STIs: No

Cost: $0 to $1,300

Usage: Lasts 3-10 years

Birth Control Implant

The implant is inserted into the upper arm by a medical professional, which releases hormones to prevent pregnancy.

Effectiveness: 99%

Prevents STIs: No

Cost: $0 to $1,300

Usage: Lasts up to 5 years

Birth Control Shot

The shot is not suitable for long-term use (2+ years) unless no other methods are viable.
